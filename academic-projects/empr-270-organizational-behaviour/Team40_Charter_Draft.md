## Team Norms and Standards

### Team norms
The values we would all like to have present in our work would be based in openness, inclusion, forward thinking, and availability to our teammates at all times. We also want direct but respectful communication, where we can ask questions early instead of assuming. This would bring forth a strong foundation for efficiency, trust, and inclusion in our study.

### Project standards
We are aiming to have a high-quality team submission that clearly applies EMPR 270 concepts and reads like one consistent paper. A realistic standard for us is work that would be in an A range: accurate concept use, clear analysis, good evidence, proper APA citations, and professional writing quality.

### What strategies do you plan to use to meet this standard?
We will use one shared source table and one shared draft document so all claims are tied to evidence from the start. We will assign one section owner for each part (context, analysis, recommendations) and one final integration editor so the voice is consistent. We will also do a rubric check 48 hours before submission and reserve the final 24 hours for polishing and citation fixes.

### Please articulate your team's intentions around engaging with AI tools, in line with the assignment instructions and course AI policy.
Our team will follow the course AI policy exactly. AI may be used only for limited tasks that are explicitly allowed in assignment instructions, such as helping identify possible organizations to research. AI will not be used to do our analysis or write our final arguments. We will fact-check everything against original sources, cite AI use where required, and ensure all submitted work is our own.

## Supporting Team Member Participation and Shared Contributions

### In week 7, we learned about the importance of psychological safety in teams - the shared sense that ideas, questions, and challenging each other is not only accepted but welcomed. What strategies can you use to support that collective sense of psychological safety?
We will support psychological safety by making space for each member to speak before decisions are finalized. During meetings, we will do quick round-robin check-ins so quieter members are invited in and not talked over. We will challenge ideas, not people, and if there is disagreement we will ask clarifying questions first. We will also treat mistakes as learning points and not as personal failures.

### How will you ensure equal distribution of tasks across the next 5 weeks, including the initial stages and final edit? What are the timelines for this work? Consider where sequential interdependence is needed vs. reciprocal.
We will distribute work through both reciprocal and sequential interdependence. In the reciprocal phase (Weeks 8-10), everyone contributes to source gathering and idea development at the same time. In the sequential phase (Weeks 10-12), context feeds into analysis, analysis feeds into recommendations, and then all sections move to one integration editor for final coherence.

Timeline:
- Week 8: Finalize charter and assign project roles.
- Week 9: Select organization and complete source evidence table.
- Week 10: Complete context draft and structure/culture concept mapping.
- Week 11: Complete analysis and recommendations draft.
- Week 12: Final integration edit, citation pass, and submission buffer.

### What strategies will you use to ensure that the project is on track? (e.g., soft deadlines, check-ins, accountability, reviewing each other's work)
We will use internal soft deadlines that are at least 2 days before actual due dates. We will run one in-person weekly meeting and one mid-week asynchronous check-in. Each task will have a named owner and backup reviewer, and unfinished tasks must be flagged early so work can be redistributed. We will use a final checklist covering concept accuracy, structure, culture, recommendations, APA, and formatting.

## Team Procedures - Communication and Meetings

### How will you communicate for updates/planning (e.g., e-mail, text, group chat (WhatsApp)? Who will set up the initial thread?
We will use a WhatsApp group chat for quick updates and email for formal deadlines and document links. Quinn will set up the initial WhatsApp thread, and Arham will send the first summary email after our first full meeting.

### When/how often will you meet over the next 5 weeks? What is our preferred time/date and medium for regular meetings? Remember that richness of medium matters!
We will meet once per week after Friday class for 30-45 minutes, in person when possible or on video call when needed. For complex decisions, conflict points, or major revisions, we will use richer channels (in-person/video) as discussed in Week 8 communication content. For routine updates, we will use leaner channels (chat/email/comments) to stay efficient.

### How will you share drafts/documents (e.g., Google Drive, Dropbox, etc). Do we need to set up a Google Drive folder or Dropbox? If so, who will take the lead on this?
We will use a shared Google Drive folder with one master Google Doc for drafting and one source/evidence tracker file. Arham will create the folder structure and sharing permissions, and all members will upload sources directly to avoid version confusion.

### Method for setting and following meeting agendas (plans)
### Who will set each agenda? When? How will team members be notified/reminded? Who will be responsible for the team following the agenda during a team meeting?
The meeting coordinator for each week will post the agenda in the chat and email at least 12 hours before the meeting. A reminder will be sent 2 hours before start time. During the meeting, the same coordinator will keep the team on the agenda and time-box each section so all priority items are covered.

### Method of record keeping and follow-up planning
### Who will be responsible for taking notes from each meeting and following up with key tasks?
We will rotate note-taking weekly so workload is shared. Meeting notes will include decisions, deadlines, owners, and blockers, and will be posted in the shared folder within 12 hours after each meeting. The next meeting coordinator will follow up on outstanding tasks at the start of the next check-in.

## Contingency Planning

### How will we handle any issues with members' participation and contributions?
If participation issues come up, we will first address them privately and respectfully with the member, focused on expectations and support. If the issue continues, we will discuss as a full team and reassign urgent tasks to protect deadlines. If there is still minimal participation, we will document the issue and contact the instructor early, with clear records of communication and missed deliverables.

### Anything else you would like to add to the team charter at this time? Feel free to use this space to articulate any elements that your team deems important!
We want this charter to be a working document, not just a one-time submission. If project conditions change, we will update this agreement together and confirm changes in writing so expectations remain clear for everyone.
